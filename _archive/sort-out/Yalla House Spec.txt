Yalla.House / Master Project Spec (v1)
 
Goal for today: A clear, ambitious, but buildable blueprint for the Yalla.House Platform:
a global, multi-country real-estate workspace that:
	•	lets owners list once and publish everywhere,
	•	lets home-hunters manage their search in one place,
	•	integrates with major property platforms as an aggregator,
	•	and includes a referral engine where normal people earn fees for bringing users onto the platform.

1. Vision & Positioning
 
1.1 Vision
Build the default workspace for property transactions worldwide.
	•	Owners list once → Yalla.House structures the data → pushes to:
	◦	major portals (Rightmove, Zoopla, Immowelt, ImmobilienScout24, Idealista, etc. over time),
	◦	direct channels (public Yalla.House pages, QR codes, WhatsApp, email).
	•	Home-hunters manage:
	◦	saved places,
	◦	bookings,
	◦	messages,
	◦	offers/applications,
	◦	surveys/inspections
	•	“Street team” & normal users can earn fees by bringing owners & home-hunters into the system.
Position:
Infrastructure, not a traditional estate agent.
We reduce admin and friction for everyone, not compete for commission.
 
1.2 Core Principles
	1.	List once, reuse everywhere
	◦	Single structured listing → multiple channels.
	◦	Internal “placeId” is stable; slugs/links can change.
	2.	Booking-first & conversation-light
	◦	Public property pages are calendar-led, not brochure led.
	◦	No dead ends; always a way to book or request a viewing.
	3.	Agents are real, NoAgent is glue
	◦	We don’t replace real agents; we centralise information, surface serious leads, and cut admin.
	◦	Agents can optionally use the platform; they’re never forced.
	4.	Global from day 1
	◦	Multi-country aware:
	▪	currencies,
	▪	units (sqm/ft²),
	▪	tax/tenure fields,
	▪	portals & practices per country.
	5.	Referral-first go-to-market
	◦	Any person can earn small but real fees by:
	▪	getting owners to create listings,
	▪	getting home-hunters to sign up and actually use the platform.

2. Core Roles & Workspaces (Phase 1)
We focus on four primary roles, all of which must be structurally supported (data model + UI):
	1.	Owner (seller or landlord)
	◦	Manages:
	▪	Owner Workspace
	▪	O1 Dashboard
	▪	O2 My Listings
	▪	O3 Inquiries & Offers
	▪	O4 Calendar
	▪	O5 Public Property Page (Preview)
	◦	Job: create & manage listings, viewings, offers/applications.
	2.	Home-Hunter (buyer or renter)
	◦	Manages:
	▪	H1 Dashboard
	▪	H2 Inbox
	▪	H3 My Bookings
	▪	H4 Saved Places
	▪	H5 Offers & Applications
	▪	H6 Survey Hub (Phase 2+)
	◦	Job: search, save, book, negotiate, and keep sanity.
	3.	Admin (Yalla.House)
	◦	Admin workspace:
	▪	A1 Dashboard (platform health)
	▪	A2 Listings (global overview)
	▪	A3 Users (owners, home-hunters, partners)
	▪	A4 Integrations
	▪	A8 Billing
	▪	A9 Analytics
	◦	Job: operate system, manage integrations, monitor costs, manage abuse.
	4.	Street Team / Referrers / Partners (Phase 1.5+)
	◦	People who:
	▪	bring owners to the platform,
	▪	bring home-hunters,
	▪	possibly provide services (photos, floorplans, viewings, translation).
	◦	They see:
	▪	Partner / Referrer dashboard (high-level: # of referred users, earnings, status of payouts).
For Claude: treat Street Team as a user type with its own table & dashboard, not just a tag.

3. High-Level Product Flows
3.1 Owner Flow (sale or rent)
	1.	Public → Landing (P1)
	◦	Sees Owner workspace preview (read-only sidebar + fake data).
	◦	Primary CTA: Create your workspace.
	2.	Auth → Owner Onboarding
	◦	Email login (or magic link).
	◦	Confirm they are an owner wanting to list.
	3.	O2 — Listing Wizard (Steps 1–5 + Publish Plan)
	◦	Step 1: Basics (address, property type, intent: for sale / to rent).
	▪	Saving Step 1 creates listing in draft with permanent internal placeId.
	◦	Step 2: Price (amount, label, shared ownership fields).
	◦	Step 3: Details (beds, baths, size, description, key features, optional country-specific fields).
	◦	Step 4: Media & docs (min. photos, floorplan optional, EPC optional).
	◦	Step 5: Checklist + Preview (O5).
	◦	+1: Publish Plan (pricing & portals).
	4.	O5 — Public Property Page (Owner Preview)
	◦	Owner sees exactly what home-hunters see.
	◦	Booking-first:
	▪	availability preview,
	▪	full calendar,
	▪	Request Viewing fallback if no slots.
	5.	Publish
	◦	Choose plan (free/paid).
	◦	Integrations triggered:
	▪	portal feeds (where possible),
	▪	QR link,
	▪	public URL ready for sharing.
	6.	Operate
	◦	O1 Dashboard → monitor funnel:
	▪	views → inquiries → bookings → offers → accepted.
	◦	O3 Inquiries & Offers:
	▪	unified messaging.
	◦	O4 Calendar:
	▪	manage availability,
	▪	confirm/cancel bookings,
	▪	sync to Google Calendar (one-way).

3.2 Home-Hunter Flow
	1.	Public → Projected from a property link
	◦	Lands on Public Property Page (P6/O5).
	◦	Wants to:
	▪	arrange viewing,
	▪	message owner,
	▪	save property.
	2.	HH Gate
	◦	Light signup:
	▪	email or phone,
	▪	basic profile (name, budget, timeline, target area).
	◦	Optional: indicate willingness to receive help from real agents (not required, but tracked).
	3.	Booking + Inbox
	◦	H3 My Bookings:
	▪	clear statuses:
	▪	Pending confirmation vs Confirmed vs Cancelled vs Completed.
	◦	H2 Inbox:
	▪	messages from owners,
	▪	viewing confirmations,
	▪	agent replies (routed via NoAgent, not direct).
	4.	Offers & Applications
	◦	H5:
	▪	send offers (sale) or applications (rent),
	▪	track status: Submitted / Under Review / Accepted / Declined.
	5.	Later: Surveys/Reports
	◦	H6:
	▪	upload & trade surveys,
	▪	buy from other users,
	▪	attach to properties.

3.3 Admin Flow (operator)
	1.	A1 Dashboard
	◦	KPIs:
	▪	active listings (sale vs rent),
	▪	active owners,
	▪	active home-hunters,
	▪	active countries,
	▪	conversion funnel.
	2.	A2 Listings
	◦	Moderation, flags, manual status changes.
	3.	A4 Integrations
	◦	Multi-country integration table:
	▪	WhatsApp, SMS, email,
	▪	portals per country,
	▪	payments (Stripe),
	▪	infra (hosting, logging),
	▪	running costs, status.
	4.	A8 Billing
	◦	Track revenue streams from:
	▪	listing plans,
	▪	services,
	▪	referral payouts (costs).

4. Integrations & Aggregator Strategy (Global Scale)
The “world domination” part relies heavily on integrations & automation.
4.1 Integration Categories
	1.	Messaging
	◦	WhatsApp API (Twilio or similar),
	◦	transactional email (Resend/SendGrid),
	◦	optional SMS.
	2.	Payments
	◦	Stripe:
	▪	one-off payments (listing packages),
	▪	recurring (subscriptions),
	▪	support multiple currencies.
	3.	Calendars
	◦	Phase 1:
	▪	one-way ICS or Google Calendar sync.
	◦	Phase 2:
	▪	Outlook, Apple, two-way options.
	4.	Portals / Major Platforms
	◦	Per country integration rows:
	▪	UK: Rightmove, Zoopla, OnTheMarket.
	▪	DE: Immowelt, ImmobilienScout24.
	▪	ES, FR, etc. later.
	◦	Initially:
	▪	manual or semi-manual feed through a feed aggregator (partner or MIT-licensed tools).
	◦	Long-term:
	▪	direct API where possible.
	5.	Analytics & Attribution
	◦	GA4, Meta Pixel, basic event tracking.
	◦	Referral tracking (see below).

4.2 Aggregator Logic (Concept)
Claude: you should turn this into a technical architecture and task list.
	•	Internal Listings table is the source of truth:
	◦	fully normalised, country-aware.
	•	Each portal has a mapping layer:
	◦	required fields,
	◦	optional fields,
	◦	transformations (e.g. sqm → ft², Freehold → OWNERSHIP_FREEHOLD).
	•	Feed logic:
	◦	Phase 1: nightly/periodic exports (e.g. XML/JSON/CSV) to feed partners.
	◦	Phase 2+: webhooks/API-based near-real-time updates.
	•	Per-country integration config:
	◦	which portals active,
	◦	credentials,
	◦	price per listing,
	◦	rules (e.g. minimum photo count, EPC requirement).

4.3 Global Scalability Considerations
Claude should design:
	•	CountryConfig data structure:
	◦	currency,
	◦	default units,
	◦	local tax/tenure fields,
	◦	default language,
	◦	active portals,
	◦	legal requirements for listing (e.g. EPC before marketing).
	•	Multi-language:
	◦	Start with English, but ensure text is key-based and translatable.
	•	Time zones:
	◦	viewings & bookings must store timestamps with timezone.

5. Referral & Street Team Engine (GTM)
We want a growth engine where normal people can earn fees:
	•	bringing Owners (listing creators),
	•	bringing Home-Hunters,
	•	possibly bringing agents & partners later.
5.1 Basic Referral Model (Phase 1)
	•	Each referrer gets a referral code / link:
	◦	e.g. yalla.house/?ref=abc123.
	•	On signup:
	◦	referrer_id stored against new user’s profile (Owner, HH, or both).
	•	Rewards (examples):
	◦	Flat fee when:
	▪	Owner completes listing (reaches Step 5 + preview).
	▪	Home-hunter verifies and completes a first booking.
	•	Payouts:
	◦	aggregated monthly (through Stripe Connect or manual transfer).
 
5.2 Street Team / Door-to-Door Use Case
This aligns with your “Street Team” concept.
	•	Street team member visits households or talks to people:
	◦	uses a simple referral app (mobile friendly web) to:
	▪	capture owner details,
	▪	trigger an email or WhatsApp to the owner with a magic link.
	◦	That magic link carries the street team referrer id.
	•	When the owner completes listing → referrer gets credit.

5.3 Referral Tracking Requirements
Claude should design tables such as:
	•	referrers
	◦	id, name (or alias), type (street team, regular user), country, payout method.
	•	referrals
	◦	id, referrer_id, referred_user_id, type (owner, hh, agent), created_at.
	•	referral_events
	◦	id, referral_id, milestone (LISTING_COMPLETED, FIRST_BOOKING, PAID_PLAN), status, value, payout_status.
 
Important constraint:
	•	No pyramid schemes, no multi-level logic.
Just simple 1-level referrer → referred user.

6. Trust, Legal & Compliance
 
We must bake in trust and compliance, not bolt it on.
 
Key points Claude should consider:
	•	No spam policy
	◦	Agents cannot spam owners/HHs via platform.
	◦	Owner/HH must always initiate or explicitly consent.
	•	GDPR & data minimisation
	◦	Collect only what’s necessary for:
	▪	bookings,
	▪	offers,
	▪	integrations.
	◦	Data processing agreements with key providers (Stripe, Twilio, etc.).
	•	NoAgent doesn’t act as “agent of record”
	◦	We are a tech platform; local legal disclaimers per country.
	•	Clear distinction:
	◦	“real agents” vs “NoAgent platform”.
	◦	Keep copy clear so users don’t get confused.
